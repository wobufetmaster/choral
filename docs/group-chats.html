Group Chats

Multi-character conversations and interactions

What are Group Chats?

Group chats let multiple AI characters participate in the same conversation, each with their own personality and perspective. Perfect for complex scenarios, character interactions, and dynamic storytelling.

Creating a Group Chat

Click Create Group Chat on the home screen, then:

  1. Select 2 or more characters from your roster
  2. Choose a strategy (see below)
  3. Click Create Group

Group Chat Strategies

Strategies determine how multiple characters interact:

Strategy Behavior Best For
Join All character descriptions are merged into the system prompt Characters who might naturally interact
Separate Character contexts are kept distinct Characters from different worlds/settings
Random AI randomly selects which character responds Chaotic, unpredictable conversations
Round Robin Characters take turns responding in order Structured discussions or debates

Example: Council of Olympus

Let's create a group chat with Athena, Hermes, Artemis, and Dionysus:

The Divine Council
You're summoned to Mount Olympus where four gods debate a mortal's fate. Each brings their unique perspective to the discussion. 
You: I seek the wisdom of the gods. A village is in peril from
a great beast. Should they fight, flee, or try to tame it?

Athena: *She steps forward, her gray eyes thoughtful* Strategy
is key. Tell us - what do you know of this beast? Its nature will
determine the wisest course.

Artemis: *Artemis's hand moves to her bow* If it threatens the
innocent, it must be stopped. But if it merely defends its territory,
the mortals are the trespassers.

Hermes: *He grins* Or they could hire me to deliver a very
polite message asking the beast to relocate! {{random:For a small fee,Cheapest solution,Everyone wins}}!

Dionysus: *Laughs heartily* Why so serious? Perhaps they should
throw a festival! Nothing soothes savage beasts like wine and music.
I speak from experience!
Group Dynamics

Characters naturally play off each other. Athena's wisdom contrasts with Dionysus's hedonism, while Artemis's directness balances Hermes's playfulness. The AI understands these dynamics from the character descriptions.

Controlling Group Conversations

Addressing Specific Characters

You can direct questions to specific characters:

Athena, what would you advise?
Hermes, can you scout ahead?
Artemis, what do you sense in the forest?

Requesting Multiple Responses

Ask multiple characters to weigh in:

What do each of you think about this?
I'd like to hear everyone's perspective.

Encouraging Interaction

Get characters talking to each other, not just to you:

Athena, what do you think of Dionysus's suggestion?
Do you two ever agree on anything?

Advanced Group Chat Techniques

Shared Lorebooks

Create a lorebook that all characters in the group can reference. Perfect for:

  • Shared world-building (locations, events, NPCs)
  • Common history or lore
  • Plot threads that affect multiple characters

Character-Specific Greetings

When starting a group chat, you can use alternate greetings for different scenarios:

  • Formal council meeting
  • Casual gathering
  • Emergency situation

Managing Large Groups

For groups of 4+ characters:

  • Use Round Robin to ensure everyone gets a turn
  • Explicitly address characters by name
  • Keep character descriptions concise to save tokens
  • Consider having some characters speak less frequently

Best Practices

Do's ✓
  • Choose characters with complementary personalities
  • Use clear names when addressing specific characters
  • Let characters disagree and create tension
  • Keep group size manageable (2-5 characters ideal)
  • Use shared lorebooks for common context
Don'ts ✗
  • Don't add too many similar characters
  • Don't expect every character to respond to every message
  • Don't forget to give each character space to shine
  • Don't use extremely long character descriptions
  • Don't be surprised by unexpected interactions!

Troubleshooting

Characters Sound Too Similar

Ensure each character has distinct speech patterns in their personality and example messages. Use different vocabulary, sentence structure, and formatting.

One Character Dominates

Try Round Robin strategy, or explicitly ask for other characters' input. You can also adjust character descriptions to emphasize when they speak vs. listen.

Characters Don't Interact

Prompt interaction: "What do you think of what [Character] just said?" or "Do you two agree?" This encourages characters to respond to each other.

Creating Group-Specific Characters

Some characters work better in groups than solo:

  • The Mediator: Asks others for opinions, facilitates discussion
  • The Provocateur: Challenges others, creates conflict
  • The Observer: Comments on group dynamics, provides meta-insights
  • The Wildcard: Unpredictable, uses {{random:}} heavily
